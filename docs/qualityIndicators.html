Quality Indicators in the SWS

Chapter 2 Quality Indicators

This chapter describes the QI to be calculated in the SWS. These indicators have been proposed by OCS and endorsed by the IDWG. Nevertheless, for a better description and information regarding the indicators below, please read the following documents.

  • the Statistical Standard Series - Quality Indicators to Be Disseminated to External Users
  • the Quality Indicators - FAO ESS review for questionnaire-based FAOSTAT domains, which contains the ready-to-use quality indicators, based on the first document.

The indicators can ne split in the eight dimensions:

  • Relevance
  • Accuracy
  • Reliability
  • Timeliness
  • Punctuality
  • Coherence
  • Comparability
  • Accessibility

We will focus on the description and implementation of two dimensions: Accuracy and Reliability. Although all quality dimensions are very important and should be taken into account to measure the quality of a statistical output, accuracy and reliability are the only ones that can be measured with the disseminated data. The remain ones are feasible to measure once the technical units provides us with information regarding their calendars/deadlines.
